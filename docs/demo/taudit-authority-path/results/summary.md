## What remains (and why it is acceptable)

The AFTER workflow is **not** zero-findings, and that is the honest result:

- **2 critical `authority_propagation` on `GITHUB_TOKEN`** — GitHub Actions
  injects `GITHUB_TOKEN` into every job. Its reach is structural; you cannot
  drive it to zero without removing all actions. This is the **floor**, not a
  regression.
- **3 high `authority_propagation`** — the three deploy secrets still reach a
  *third-party* action. It is now pinned and reviewed, so this is residual
  third-party-trust risk, not an open exploit. Reducing it further means
  swapping the secrets for OIDC or a first-party deploy path.
- **1 high `over_privileged_identity`** — `id-token: write` on deploy could be
  scoped tighter still.

These residuals are exactly what the **baseline** step records as the accepted
floor (see [`../baseline/`](../baseline)). `taudit baseline diff` reports
`0 NEW` against this baseline, so future pull requests are gated against *new*
authority movement, not against the known, accepted floor.
